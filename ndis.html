Understanding the NDIS

New to the National Disability Insurance Scheme? Here's a simple, jargon-free guide - and we're always happy to help you in person.

The basics

What is the NDIS?

The National Disability Insurance Scheme (NDIS) is Australia's way of funding support for people with permanent and significant disability. Rather than a one-size-fits-all approach, it gives you an individual plan and budget to purchase the supports and services that help you pursue your goals and live more independently.

As a registered provider, One Support Disability can deliver supports from your plan and help you navigate the system with confidence.

Eligibility

Am I eligible?

Generally, you may be able to access the NDIS if you:

  • Are aged between 7 and 65 when you apply
  • Are an Australian citizen or permanent resident
  • Have a permanent and significant disability
  • Need support from a person or equipment to do daily tasks

Children under 7 may be supported through the early childhood approach. Not sure if you qualify? Get in touch and we'll point you in the right direction.

Your plan

The three NDIS budgets

Your funding is grouped into three types of support.

Core Supports

Everyday assistance - daily living, personal care, transport and community participation. The most flexible budget.

Capacity Building

Building your skills and independence - therapies, support coordination, training and employment supports.

Capital Supports

Higher-cost items - assistive technology, equipment and home or vehicle modifications.

Getting started

How to access the NDIS

Check your eligibility

Confirm you meet the access requirements, or contact the NDIS on 1800 800 110.

Make an access request

Submit your request with evidence of your disability from your treating professionals.

Plan & choose providers

Once approved, you'll create a plan - then choose providers like us to deliver your supports.
